Federal Public Service Commission

General Knowledge-III (Pakistan Affairs)

Competitive Examination-2018

The 2018 Pakistan Affairs paper reflects a mix of ideological history and contemporary governance challenges. Early questions focus on ideological foundations and partition plans, while later ones emphasize constitutional structure, centre–province relations, national integration, and security issues like non-state actors. The paper also highlights foreign policy through Pakistan–US relations, indicating a trend of linking historical ideology with modern political and security challenges.


PART-I (MCQs)
1. The convergence point of the five rivers of Punjab in Pakistan is:

A. Nawab Muzzaffar Ali | B. Mirza Mumtaz Hassan | C. Abdul Jabbar Khan | D. Nawab Akbar Bugti

A. Jawahar Lal Nehru | B. Mahatma Gandhi | C. Ram Mohan Roy | D. None of these
5. When were the elections for the second Constituent National Assembly held?
A. 23 March, 1956 | B. 21 June, 1955 | C. 14 August, 1957 | D. 23 October, 1958

PART-II (Subjective)

Question No. 2

Critically evaluate the foundations of Allama Iqbal’s concept of Muslim separatism in the context of Indo-Pak Subcontinent.

Question No. 3

Discuss the 3rd June Plan or Mountbatten Plan and also describe how it was implemented?

Question No. 4

Describe the political system of Pakistan. Which form of government is suitable to make country politically stable?

Question No. 5

Define non-state actors. ‘Non-state actors are posing more threat to Pakistan’s national security than threats from external state actors.’ Argue in favour or against.

Question No. 6

Discuss the nature of centre-province relations under the 1973 Constitution.

Question No. 7

Discuss the changing trends in Pakistan- US relations since 2001.

Question No. 8

There is crisis of national integration in Pakistan since its creation. What measures do you suggest for the promotion of national integration of Pakistan?
