1. Antibiotics are used to fight against:
A) Proteins | B) Viruses | C) Bacteria | D) None of these
2. An astronomical unit (AU) or light year (LY) is used to measure:
A) Distance | B) Time period | C) Light intensity | D) Time
3. Radio waves broadcasted by radio stations are reflected back to Earth by the:
A) Ionosphere | B) Stratosphere | C) Mesosphere | D) None of these
4. Failing to finish a full course of antibiotics often results in:
A) Some bacteria left un-killed | B) Production of more bacteria | C) Bacteria becoming sensitive | D) Bacteria becoming resistant
5. During combustion of petrol in a car engine, chemical energy is converted into:
A) Chemical | B) Mechanical | C) Electrical | D) None of these
6. Proteins are essentially made up of chains called:
A) Polynucleotide | B) Polypeptide | C) Oxyacetylene | D) None of these
7. A barometer is an instrument used for measuring:
A) Temperature | B) Humidity | C) Pressure | D) None of these
8. In radar, we use radio waves; whereas in sonar, we use:
A) Red waves | B) Infrared waves | C) Ultrasonic waves | D) Supersonic waves
9. An ionic bond between two atoms involves the:
A) Complete transfer of valence electrons | B) Half transfer of valence electrons | C) Complete transfer of electrons | D) None of these
10. Dengue fever is specifically transmitted by the:
A) Bite of an infected mosquito | B) Bite of an infected male Aedes | C) Bite of an infected female Anopheles | D) Bite of an infected female Aedes mosquito
11. The sky appears blue primarily because of:
A) Rayleigh scattering | B) Reflection | C) Mie scattering | D) Absorption
12. Cyclones are weather phenomena that:
A) Experience Coriolis effects | B) Are super geotropic | C) Are fair weather | D) Are associated with low-pressure systems
13. Rainbow formation is the result of:
A) Absorption | B) Diffusion | C) Ionization | D) Refraction and reflection of sunlight by water droplets

A) Exosphere | B) Mesosphere | C) Troposphere | D) Stratosphere

A) An astronomical unit (AU) | B) Neither | C) They are the same | D) A light-year (LY)
16. The direction in which the Earth rotates on its axis is:
A) West to east | B) East to west | C) North to south | D) South to north
17. A collection of water droplets suspended in the air is called a:
A) Wind | B) Cloud | C) Gases | D) Sleet
18. To lose weight, a common general recommendation for daily calorie intake is:
A) 2000 | B) 2500 | C) 1500 | D) 3000
19. The precise time for Earth to complete one rotation (Sidereal Day) is:
A) 22 hours | B) 23 hours | C) 23 hours 56 minutes | D) None of these
20. Most enzymes found in the human body are chemically classified as:
A) Proteins | B) Viruses | C) Bacteria | D) None of these

SECTION-II

Question No. 6

(A) It takes 3 liters of paint to cover an area of 24 square meters. What percentage increase in the quantity of paint would be required to cover an area of 50.4 square meters?
(B) An automobile travels 3 km towards south, 4 km towards west, 5 km towards north and 2 km towards south-east. How far is it from its starting position?
(C) Tahir started a business with a capital of Rs. 15,000. After 5 months Umar also joined him with an investment of Rs. 30,000. At the start of 9th month, Usman joined them by investing Rs. 45,000. At the end of the year they earned a profit of Rs. 406,000. Find the share of each one.
(D) A man left his property of Rs. 640,000. A debt of Rs. 40,000 was due to him and Rs. 5,000 was spent on his burial. Distribute the amount between his widow, one daughter and two sons according to the Islamic Law.
