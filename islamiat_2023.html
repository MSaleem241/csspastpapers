Islamic Studies / Comparative Religions

Competitive Examination-2023

The 2023 Islamiat paper emphasizes Islamic legal principles, social justice, and contemporary societal challenges. Questions cover core concepts such as the Hereafter, Ijma, and the Charter of Madina, while also addressing issues like women’s rights and extremism in Pakistan. The paper reflects a consistent trend of linking classical Islamic teachings with modern governance, social harmony, and ethical responsibilities.

PART-II (Subjective)

Question No. 2

What is the Qur’anic argument on the Life Hereafter? What are its impacts on the individual and collective life of a human being?

Question No. 3

Discuss the Holy Prophet’s (P.B.U.H) Role as a Model for Military Strategy.

Question No. 4

Elaborate the rights of daughters granted by Islam and how these are denied by Muslims in the contemporary world?

Question No. 5

Define Ijma‘(consensus) and explain its different kinds. Can legislation by a parliament of an Islamic state be regarded as a valid consensus?

Question No. 6

Analyze the Charter of Madina as a social contract.

Question No. 7

What are the motives of extremism in Pakistan? How can the society get rid of extremism by following the teachings of Islam?

Question No. 8

Write notes on the following:
(i) Human rights in the light of the Sermon of Farewell Pilgrimage.
(ii) Social Justice in an Islamic Society.
